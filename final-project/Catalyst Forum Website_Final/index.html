Independent Schools

Planning for a New Reality

Are you an independent school leader faced with the challenges of responding to recent facility, economic, and demographic shifts?

Since the economic downturn there have been a variety of factors that have shifted the characteristics of American households. Maintaining and increasing enrollment amidst these rapid changes is often requiring school leaders to make changes to the enrollment strategy and compels a comprehensive analysis of how both the delivery of education and the physical assets of the campus drive targeted outcomes that align with these new strategic objectives.


Join us in this collaborate workshop to discuss ideas on how to build a forward-thinking and mission-driven strategy through renewed educational delivery and capital campaigns.

Tom Murphy

Senior Fellow, Urban Land Institute

During his 12-year tenure as mayor of Pittsburgh, Mr. Murphy initiated a P3 strategy that leveraged more than $4.5 billion in economic development in the city. His presentation will focus on the economic impact universities have on local economies and detailed strategies that can be employed to facilitate P3s.

India Pierce Lee

Program Director, The Cleveland Foundation

Ms. Lee will present the “Greater Circle Living” program, a groundbreaking program of financial incentives for employees to live close to work in the Greater University Circle area in Cleveland. She will describe methods used for engaging employers and the policy / program design work.

Tom Calhoun

Vice President, George Mason University

Mr. Calhoun will present on his experience implementing Masonvale, the ULI award-winning employer-assisted housing development, which George Mason University pursued to support the recruitment and retention of faculty and to provide workforce housing for city/county employees.

Chris Dunlavey

President, Brailsford & Dunlavey

Mr. Dunlavey specializes in managing the development of anchors of community such as PK-12 schools, major sports venues, and higher education “quality of life” projects. He will moderate the event, including the facilitiation of the roundtable discussions.
